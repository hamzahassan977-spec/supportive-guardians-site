The information we collect

When you book, sign in to the client portal, or complete the intake form, we may collect:

  • Your name, date of birth, email address and phone number.
  • Your GP name and surgery, and an emergency contact's name, phone number and relationship to you.
  • What you would like support with, how long it has been going on, any previous support, and any medication you choose to tell us about.
  • Your answer to a short wellbeing and safety question, and anything else you choose to share.
  • Your booking and session details, and the consents you give on the form.

Some of this is sensitive information about your health and wellbeing. We treat it with extra care, and you only ever share what you are comfortable sharing.

How and why we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Provide the coaching you have asked for, prepare for sessions, and stay in touch with you about them.
  • Keep accurate, confidential records of our work together.
  • Respond to your enquiries and manage bookings and payments.
  • Keep you and others safe, and meet our legal and safeguarding responsibilities.

Our lawful bases under UK GDPR are: performing our agreement with you to deliver coaching; your explicit consent for the health and wellbeing details you share; our legitimate interest in running a safe, well managed practice; protecting someone's vital interests where there is a serious risk to safety; and meeting legal obligations where they apply. Where we rely on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

Keeping you safe

What you share is confidential. The main exception is where we believe there is a serious risk to your safety or someone else's. In that situation we may share relevant information with your GP, emergency services, or the appropriate safeguarding team, to help keep people safe. Wherever possible we will talk to you about this first.

Wellbeing coaching is not a clinical or emergency service, and the intake form is not monitored in real time. If you are in crisis, call 999, or Samaritans free any time on 116 123.

Who we share your information with

We do not sell your information or use it for advertising. We use a small number of trusted providers to run the service securely, who process information on our behalf under contract:

  • A scheduling provider, to manage bookings.
  • A secure database and client portal provider, to store your records.
  • An email provider, to send confirmations and notifications.

Some of these providers may process data outside the UK. Where they do, we rely on appropriate safeguards, such as standard contractual clauses, to protect your information.

How long we keep it

We keep your information for as long as you are a client and for a set period afterwards, currently [RETENTION PERIOD, e.g. up to 7 years] after our last contact, after which we securely delete it. If you ask us to delete your information sooner, we will do so unless we are required to keep it for legal or safeguarding reasons.

How we protect your information

Your records are stored in a secure database with access controls, so only your practitioner can see them. Information is encrypted in transit, access is restricted, and we keep our systems up to date. We limit what we collect to what we genuinely need.

Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to:

  • Ask for a copy of the information we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct anything that is wrong or incomplete.
  • Ask us to delete your information, or to restrict or object to how we use it.
  • Ask us to transfer your information to another provider.
  • Withdraw your consent at any time, where we rely on it.

To exercise any of these, email Saidaaden@supportiveguardians.com. We will respond within one month.

Cookies

This site uses only what is needed to work and to load our booking tool. We do not use advertising or tracking cookies. Our booking provider may set its own cookies when you open the booking window, which are covered by that provider's own privacy notice.
